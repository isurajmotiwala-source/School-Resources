1. Teacher Arrival & Reporting

The school day begins here. Being on time sets the tone for students and colleagues.

7:50 am – 8:00 am  ·  Reporting after 8:00 am is considered late
✓ Do
  • Arrive before 8:00 am and input attendance
  • Prepare teaching materials and the classroom
  • Ensure the classroom is clean and properly arranged
  • Maintain a professional, welcoming attitude
  • Be ready to guide students as they enter campus
✗ Don't
  • Arrive after the reporting window
  • Begin the day unprepared or rushed
  • Use mobile phones during arrival time
  • Leave students unattended in classrooms
  • Ignore early discipline or safety concerns
🚪
Gate Incharge

2. Gate Duty — Entry & Exit

Ensures safe, orderly movement of students during arrival and dispersal.

8:00 am – 8:10 am (Entry)  ·  Dispersal time as directed
✓ Do
  • Reach the gate on time
  • Ensure students move safely and calmly — no crowding
  • Greet parents and students warmly; model respectful greetings
  • Randomly check bags in a friendly manner
  • Report unusual or unsafe situations to HOD immediately
✗ Don't
  • Leave the gate unattended
  • Allow pushing, running, or disorderly behaviour
  • Engage in long personal conversations
  • Ignore students who require assistance
  • Use mobile phones while supervising
🎙
All Teachers · Class Teacher in Charge

3. Morning Assembly

Builds discipline, public speaking, and a positive start to the day.

Secondary: 8:10 – 8:25 am  ·  Primary: 8:55 – 9:10 am
📅 Combined Assembly
  • Held every Monday and during important school events. All classes assemble together as instructed.
📅 Class Assembly
  • Held Tuesday to Friday, within the classroom. A different class monitor is assigned each day.
💡 Rotate student monitors regularly. Every student should get opportunities to lead over time.

The monitor leads the class through this sequence from the front:

  1. Students stand properly in their places
  2. Monitor gives commands: "Attention" and "Stand at Ease"
  3. Prayer is conducted
  4. Pledge is recited
  5. Affirmations — read by monitor, repeated by class
  6. News Reading — a student shares current news
  7. Proverb of the Day — a student reads and briefly explains a proverb
✓ Do
  • Ensure students stand in orderly, disciplined lines
  • Guide the student monitor conducting assembly
  • Encourage confident and clear student speaking
  • Maintain silence and attentiveness throughout
  • Ensure assembly proceeds smoothly and respectfully
✗ Don't
  • Talk with other teachers during assembly
  • Allow students to move unnecessarily
  • Ignore students who are disengaged or disruptive
  • Allow the same students to participate every day
  • Use phones or appear distracted

6. Recess Supervision

Two inspectors for Grades 5–10 and two for Grades 1–4 during each recess.

Recess 1: 10:15 – 10:40 am  ·  Recess 2: 12:20 – 12:40 pm
✓ Do
  • Supervise language discipline — English only
  • Prevent shouting, fighting, or unsafe play
  • Ensure students do not litter; make them pick up litter around them

7. Closing Class Assembly

End the day with clear homework, a clean classroom, and a calm, orderly exit.

2:20 pm – 2:50 pm (30 minutes)
2:20–2:30

📝 Homework (10 min)

Write homework clearly on the board. Students copy into homework notebook. Explain the task and how to complete it. Confirm all students have copied correctly.

Homework is assigned on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday only.
2:30–2:40

🧹 Cleaning & Organisation (10 min)

Assigned cleaning teams sweep the floor, dust surfaces, clean the board, arrange furniture, and clean windows. Goal: a dust-free, organized classroom for tomorrow.

2:40–2:50

🙏 Prayer, Pack-Up & Exit (10 min)

Closing prayer → calm bag packing → verify all belongings → orderly, supervised exit.

Before leaving, ensure the classroom meets all of the following:

  • Desks and chairs properly arranged
  • Whiteboard cleaned
  • Curtains opened
  • Classroom swept and dusted
  • Assigned students switch off fans and lights
✓ Do
  • Reflect briefly on the day with students
  • Share reminders and important notices
  • Ensure students pack belongings properly
  • Leave the classroom clean and orderly
  • Dismiss students calmly
✗ Don't
  • Rush the closing process
  • Ignore classroom cleanliness
  • Allow chaotic packing or movement
  • Leave without preparing the room
  • Dismiss students in an unorganized way
📝
Class Teacher — Grades 1–10

8. Homework Guidelines

Consistent, fair, and well-tracked homework builds strong study habits.

Homework is assigned three times a week: Monday, Wednesday, Friday.

Weekend

Notebooks collected & checked
✓ Do
  • Ensure students carry homework notebooks daily
  • Give clear, complete instructions for every task
  • Check homework regularly and carefully
  • Encourage neat and complete work
  • Return notebooks promptly after checking
✗ Don't
  • Give homework without explaining it
  • Assign unclear or incomplete instructions
  • Assign excessive homework
  • Allow notebooks to be mixed between students
  • Take homework notebooks home for checking
⚠️ All checking must be done within school premises. If a homework notebook is lost while in a teacher's custody, the teacher will be fined. Track collected notebooks carefully.

Maintain a Homework Completion Record Sheet for your class.

✓ Do
  • Record which students completed homework each day
  • Mark missing or incomplete homework clearly
  • Update records regularly and accurately
  • Use records to identify patterns of missing work
  • Report repeated non-submission to administration
✗ Don't
  • Skip updating the record sheet
  • Rely on memory instead of written records
  • Ignore repeated missing homework

9. Student Exit & Dispersal

Staggered timings decided by the HOD to ensure calm corridors and safe pick-up.

✓ Do
  • Ensure students leave in an orderly manner
  • Guide students toward correct exits
  • Monitor corridor discipline
  • Ensure safe pick-up where required
  • Report any safety concerns immediately
✗ Don't
  • Allow running or pushing during exit
  • Leave duty areas early
  • Ignore corridor discipline issues
  • Allow students to wander in school after dismissal
  • Dismiss students without supervision
